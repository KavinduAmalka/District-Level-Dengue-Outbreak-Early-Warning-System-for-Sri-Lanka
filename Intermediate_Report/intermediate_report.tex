% --- 1. INTRODUCTION & PROJECT OVERVIEW ---
\section{Introduction \& Project Overview}

Dengue fever remains a persistent and highly critical public health challenge in Sri Lanka. As outlined in our initial proposal, the reactive nature of current dengue outbreak management strategies highlights the urgent need for a proactive, predictive warning system. The primary goal of this research is to build a district-level early warning and risk-mapping system that forecasts cases 4 weeks in advance, enabling timely vector control interventions by Public Health Inspectors (PHIs) and optimized medical resource allocation.

\subsection{Research Objectives}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Data Fusion:} Integrate historical cases, meteorological factors, commuter mobility, and PHI larval report datasets.
    \item \textbf{Mobility Modeling:} Incorporate bus/train transit flows to predict inter-district disease propagation patterns.
    \item \textbf{Dual-Stream Modeling:} Implement Regression (case forecasting) and Classification (risk-level mapping) machine learning pipelines.
    \item \textbf{Regional Adaptation:} Address the geographical variations between Dry and Wet zones.
    \item \textbf{Decision-Support Interface:} Deliver an interactive dashboard for field workers and health authorities.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Preprocessing and Feature Engineering}
Data cleaning and aligning are being performed in Python using Pandas. Key achievements in this phase include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Lag Analysis:} Establishing time-lag features (ranging from 1 to 8 weeks) for weather indicators (rainfall, temperature, and humidity) to capture delayed mosquito vector breeding responses.
    \item \textbf{Zone Segmentation:} Annotating districts under Wet/Dry zone classifications to account for ecological and monsoon differences (Southwest vs. Northeast monsoons).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Preliminary Modeling}
We have initialized our baseline model training scripts. We are evaluating:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{LSTM Networks:} For capturing temporal patterns and long-term sequential dependencies in dengue cases.
    \item \textbf{XGBoost \& LightGBM:} For tree-based ensemble learning incorporating tabular meteorological lags and regional indicator variables.
\end{itemize}

% --- 3. CHALLENGES & MITIGATION ---
\section{Key Challenges \& Mitigation Strategies}

During the initial phase, the team encountered a few challenges:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Data Inconsistencies:} Minor gaps and reporting delays in weekly epidemiological reports. \\
    \textit{Mitigation:} We applied linear interpolation and seasonal imputation techniques to ensure continuity in time-series training.
    \item \textbf{Mobility Data Sparsity:} Lack of high-granularity mobile GPS logs for mobility indexing. \\
    \textit{Mitigation:} We are approximating inter-district commuter flows using public transit frequency datasets and gravity models of population density.
\end{itemize}
