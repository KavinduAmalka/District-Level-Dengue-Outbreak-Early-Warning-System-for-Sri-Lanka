% --- 2. PROBLEM STATEMENT ---
\section{Problem Statement}

\subsection{The Core Challenge: Reactive vs. Proactive}
Dengue management in Sri Lanka is largely reactive, triggering interventions only after infection rates spike. While countries like Singapore and Brazil use Machine Learning (ML) to anticipate outbreaks, Sri Lanka lacks a localized, proactive early warning system that provides the necessary lead time for effective prevention.

\subsection{Key Gaps and Challenges}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Data Fragmentation:} Existing models rely primarily on rainfall, failing to integrate multidimensional factors like Human Mobility and PHI Larval Surveillance data for high-accuracy predictions.
    \item \textbf{Geographical Oversimplification:} Literature often overlooks the distinct dengue-climate dynamics between Wet-Zone and Dry-Zone districts and the separate impacts of the Southwest and Northeast monsoons.
    \item \textbf{Lead-Time Inefficiency:} Without a district-specific lag analysis, Public Health Inspectors (PHIs) cannot time their vector control interventions precisely, resulting in inefficient resource allocation.
    \item \textbf{Technical Limitations:} Traditional models (e.g., ARIMA) struggle with the non-linear temporal patterns of long-term data. Advanced architectures like LSTM and XGBoost are essential to model these complex 7-year longitudinal relationships.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Proposed Solution: Actionable Prevention}
This research introduces a Dual-Stream Intelligence framework integrating human mobility and PHI larval reports with micro-climatic data. By utilizing LSTM and XGBoost architectures, the system converts complex epidemiological patterns into a 4-week-ahead predictive dashboard. This tool transforms raw data into actionable insights, empowering health authorities to transition from crisis management to proactive strategic prevention.

% --- 4. PRELIMINARY LITERATURE REVIEW ---
\section{Preliminary Literature Review}

\subsection{Existing Research and Key Findings}
Existing studies on dengue forecasting in Sri Lanka primarily emphasize the correlation between climatic variables and disease transmission. Researchers such as Malavige et al. (2021) and various epidemiological white papers identify rainfall, temperature, and relative humidity as the dominant drivers of Aedes mosquito breeding cycles.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Methodologies \& Outcomes:} Current literature strongly advocates for Deep Learning architectures, specifically Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, due to their superior ability to model non-linear temporal patterns compared to traditional statistical methods like ARIMA.
    \item \textbf{Strengths:} Existing approaches excel at establishing high-precision correlations between meteorological data and immediate outbreak peaks, often utilizing satellite-derived environmental data.
    \item \textbf{Weaknesses:} A significant limitation is that most research remains theoretical or limited to mathematical modeling, with a distinct lack of functional, user-centric software tools or dashboards. Furthermore, there is a prominent "Western Province Bias," where findings from Colombo are often generalized to other ecological zones with different transmission dynamics.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Research Gap and Justification}
A critical review of over 20 scholarly sources identifies a major gap in transforming complex predictive models into actionable public health tools that can be used for real-time decision-making.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Research Gaps:} Current research frequently isolates climatic factors, neglecting the combined socio-temporal impact of human mobility and localized biological larval indices. Additionally, there is no unified, software-driven dashboard that visualizes 4-week lead-time forecasts for multi-province surveillance.
    \item \textbf{Addressing the Gaps:} Our research addresses these deficiencies by developing an \textbf{Integrated Predictive Dashboard}. Diverging from island-wide generalizations, we adopt a localized approach by selecting \textbf{one critical district from each of the nine provinces} for granular spatio-temporal analysis.
    \item \textbf{Justification and Divergence:} This study is necessary to provide health authorities with a proactive resource allocation tool. By analyzing a comprehensive weekly panel dataset since 2017, our work builds on proven LSTM frameworks while incorporating mobility proxies and larval density reports to ensure the system is relevant to modern epidemiological trends.
\end{itemize}
